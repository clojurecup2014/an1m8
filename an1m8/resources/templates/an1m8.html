Little bit of motion could be a huge difference

Animated SVGs had been there for a while, but they have some limitations:

  • no randomness — your animations will always be the same
  • auto-generated SVGs contain far too many groups/elements — animating them is a huge pain
  • poor support for some browsers — IE, I am looking at you!

These limitaions could be fixed with the help of clojurescript and core.async. At least we hope so)

Core.async works in the IE, which supports inlined svg. Clojurescript has got enough power to deal with huge number of SVG elements and, of course, it has got a randomizer)

As a proof of this concept, we've animated our logo using this technique. I guess you've noticed that.

We've implemented some other neat animations, you can check them in gallery. Or try creating your own animation by uploading SVG.
